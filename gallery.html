Unified Electronic Medical Records & Health Management System

Developed by IICT

A unified electronic medical records and health management system is an integrated digital platform that combines patient medical records with tools for managing healthcare services all in one place.

Instead of having separate systems for patient files, appointments, billing, lab results, and hospital operations, a unified system brings everything together so healthcare providers can access and manage information seamlessly.

What it includes

At its core, it merges two major components:

  • Electronic Medical Records (EMR): Digital versions of patients' clinical data—diagnoses, prescriptions, lab results, medical history, imaging, etc.
  • Health Management System (HMS): Administrative and operational tools for appointments, billing, staff management, pharmacy, inventory, reporting.

Key features

A unified system typically offers:

  • Centralized patient records (accessible across departments or facilities)
  • Appointment scheduling and patient tracking
  • E-prescriptions and medication management
  • Lab and diagnostic integration
  • Billing, insurance, and financial management
  • Reporting and analytics for decision making
  • Role-based access for doctors, nurses, admins, etc.
